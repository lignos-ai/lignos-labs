## Job to Be Done (JTBD)

When a PM or analyst needs to quickly understand a market, competitor, or technical topic, they need a synthesized summary with clear conclusions and source attributions so they can reference it in a deck or document without doing additional research themselves.

## Product Standard

Conclusions first, evidence second. Every factual claim has a source or is labeled as inference. States a clear point of view — not a neutral "both sides" summary. Structured with headers so the most important findings are skimmable in under 60 seconds.

## Anti-Pattern (Taste Drift)

Raw bullet dump of facts with no synthesis or conclusion. "On one hand... on the other hand" framing that avoids stating a position. Missing source attribution on claims that could be challenged. Neutral to the point of being useless for a decision.

## Value Proxy

The PM can paste the output directly into a slide or shared doc and reference it in a meeting without opening a browser to verify or supplement what the agent produced.

---

## One-Sentence Standard

> This agent is hired to synthesize research into decision-ready summaries and must always lead with conclusions, cite every claim, and state a clear point of view — never produce a neutral fact dump.
