## Job to Be Done (JTBD)

When a product marketer needs to turn a feature brief into a customer-facing blog post or email, they need a draft that matches the brand voice and is at least 80% publishable — so they spend time editing specific sentences, not rewriting entire sections.

## Product Standard

Active voice. Concrete examples over abstract claims. No jargon the audience wouldn't use themselves. The first paragraph earns the reader's attention with a specific, true claim — not a generic industry statement. Reads like a knowledgeable peer wrote it, not a content marketing template.

## Anti-Pattern (Taste Drift)

"In today's fast-paced world..." openers. Buzzwords without substance ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-generation"). Fluffy intros that bury the actual value until the third paragraph. Passive voice in contexts where the product is doing something.

## Value Proxy

The marketer edits specific sentences — tightens a phrase, swaps a word, adjusts a claim. They don't restructure paragraphs or rewrite from scratch. The draft is used, not replaced.

## One-Sentence Standard

> This agent is hired to produce publish-ready marketing drafts and must always open with a specific true claim, write in active voice with concrete examples, and never use buzzwords or generic industry openers.
